Home page

        Many vegan foods are rich in plant-based protein, which doesn't have the harmful health effects of animal-derived protein. 
        Given how healthy plant-based foods can be, it's no wonder that more and more world-class athletes and other famous names
        are opting to eat vegan. Check out some of the health benefits below: 

        1. Vegan foods are rich in the nutrients your body needs. 

        2. Eating vegan helps reduce our risk of suffering from cancer and other diseases. 

        3. Vegan food can boost your mood. 

        4. Eating vegan can help you achieve a healthy body weight. 

        5. It can help prevent type 2 diabetes. 

        6. Your skin may benefit, too. 

        7. Eating vegan can reduce the pain of arthritis. 

        Jeffrey Soble, MD, a cardiologist at Rush University Medical Center, began eating a plant-based diet nearly two year ago. 

        Like others, improving his health — he has a family history of heart disease — played a significant role in that decision.
        Research has shown that a vegan diet can help do the following: 

        -Promote weight loss
        -Reduce your risk of heart disease by lowering cholesterol levels 
        -Lower your chances of getting certain types of cancer, such as colon cancer       
        -Manage diabetes by lowering A1C levels

        Tips when making the change: 

        Talk to your partner first.
        Explain your reasons for making the change. Your partner may decide to make the journey with you, or you may have to go it alone.
        If you do choose to eat differently, discuss how that will work in terms of shopping, meal preparation and dining out. 

        Take notes. 
        Write down which vegan foods you've enjoyed in the past and where to get them. This can include prepared meals from
        your local grocer, your preferred brand of soy yogurt or tasty entrées at nearby restaurants. This handy list will help
        with last-minute meal planning, and help take the stress out of shopping and dining. 

        Understand your own expectations. 
        Will you ensure that every item you eat has no animal products by reading ingredient labels? Or will you take a different
        approach and have a little ice cream at birthday parties? Veganism doesn't have to be all or nothing. Take a close look 
        at your goals and objectives and do what works best for you. "By creating your own roadmap and being realistic about 
        what you want to achieve, you'll be more satisfied with making the change," Soble says.

Recipes

          NUTRITIONAL BARS:

          Ingredients:

          - 2 ½ cups of oatmeal
          - 2 tbsp. Natural sweetener (erythritol, monk fruit)
          - ¼ cup grated coconut
          - ¼ cup chopped almonds
          - ¼ cup of peanuts
          - ¼ cup pumpkin seeds
          - ¼ cup of plum in pieces.
          - ½ cup of blueberries
          - Dark Chocolate
          - 1/3 cup coconut oil
          - ½ cup honey or agave
          - Suggestion: raisins, sunflower seeds, nuts..

          ELABORATION:

          - Mix all the ingredients very well, previously mix the liquids and add to the solid.
          - Empty on a tray that has a border, line with parchment paper.
          - Pour the mixture and compact.
          - Bake for 30 to 35 min.
          - Let cool and cut into rectangles.

          VEGAN PANCAKES:

          Ingredients:

          - 1 cup of oatmeal
          - 2 tablespoons ground flaxseed
          - 1 tablespoon sesame or sesame
          - ¾ cup non-dairy milk
          - ½ tablespoon of baking powder
          - 1 ct. Vanilla extract
          - Red fruits, blueberries.
          - Honey bees or maple.

          Elaboration:

          - Mix the ingredients and make the pancakes over coconut oil.
          - When removing from the pan cover with honey and fruits

Courses

          Vegan and integral pastry and bakery course.

          This course is designed for people who want to take care of their health with a natural and organic diet,
          it also helps a lot in the diet of people with diabetes or gluten intolerance. Among the different things 
          you will learn are the following:

          -Different protein flours, such as chickpea flour, coconut, almond, oatmeal, buckwheat, rye.
          -Produce oils that are essential for good nutrition.
          -The use of natural sweeteners, such as powdered or liquid stevia
          -Different types of recipes and preparation of vegan and wholemeal meals and desserts.
